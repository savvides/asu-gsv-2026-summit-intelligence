# What It Means to Be an American — and What It Requires of Us at This Moment

## Executive Summary

This panel explores the state of American democracy and civic education 250 years after the nation's founding, featuring Eric Liu (Citizen University), Ellen Dollarhide McCoy (Ronald Reagan Institute), Louise Dubé (iCivics), and Julie Lammers (Britebound). Eric Liu frames America's condition as "damaged and resilient," arguing the country must close the gap between its stated creed and actual deeds. The panelists share alarming data: very little K-12 civics is being taught, 35% of teachers changed lesson plans due to fear of backlash, and 20% considered quitting. Key solutions discussed include iCivics' democratic classroom model, Citizen University's "Civic Saturdays" (faith-gathering-style civic rituals), the Reagan Institute's bipartisan convening approach, and Britebound's framing of civics education as career readiness. The group agrees that the distinction between free expression and free exchange is critical -- Americans are sounding off everywhere but have lost the muscle for constructive dialogue across difference.

## Key Quotes

1. **Eric Liu:** "The state of our democracy, the state of our union, I would say is damaged and resilient. It's not a but, it's more of an and."
2. **Eric Liu:** "Free speech is not atrophying. People are sounding off everywhere. But free exchange is completely atrophying."
3. **Louise Dubé (iCivics):** "Over 35% of educators say they actually did change their lesson plans in the last year because of fear of backlash. And this is not about teaching about Iran. This is about teaching about the three branches of government."
4. **Ellen Dollarhide McCoy:** "There's no debate if everyone agrees. And so we work to bring folks from both the left and the right, from both the arts and the sciences, to the stage to produce a plurality of viewpoints."
5. **Julie Lammers:** "We talk a lot about things that are happening to youth. We don't actually ask them what it is they want, how they want to participate in the world."
