# EdTech Scouting Report: ASU+GSV 2026

**Companies and trends observed at the ASU+GSV Summit, April 13-15, 2026**

Philippos Savvides, Head of ScaleU

---

## What I Saw at the Summit

This year's ASU+GSV confirmed that AI in education has moved past the "will it happen" phase and into a crowded race around execution. The AI tutoring space, in particular, is consolidating fast: multiple companies now offer Socratic-method tutoring with similar interfaces and similar claims. The differentiators are emerging not in the tutoring itself but in what wraps around it -- integration depth with existing LMS infrastructure, subject-specific pedagogical design, and whether the product can survive scrutiny from academic integrity offices. The most interesting companies I met were the ones solving problems adjacent to tutoring rather than competing directly within it.

Two areas felt genuinely new. First, assessment in the age of AI is surfacing as a real gap -- institutions are struggling with how to evaluate student understanding when written assignments are no longer reliable signals, and a few companies are building specifically for that problem. Second, the retention and student-success space is shifting from alert-based systems (flagging at-risk students for human follow-up) toward autonomous action, where AI agents initiate interventions directly. On the instructional design side, the tools are maturing quickly, with AI now handling course scaffolding, content migration across LMS platforms, and FERPA-compliant deployment -- work that used to consume months of faculty time.

## AI Tutoring and Learning Support

**StudyStream** -- Virtual study rooms using body-doubling and social accountability to drive study behavior. What caught my attention is that this is not another AI tutor; it attacks the motivation and habit-formation layer that tutoring products assume is already solved. *Region: United Kingdom.*

**Aristotle** -- AI tutoring platform combining Socratic dialogue with interactive whiteboard, targeting gateway courses in math, general chemistry, and introductory economics. The gateway-course focus is smart -- these are the highest-volume, highest-failure courses where institutions are most willing to experiment. *Region: United States.*

**MegaMinds** -- AI-driven simulations designed around productive struggle and active engagement. Currently focused on K-12 but actively exploring higher-ed applications. The emphasis on simulation-based learning rather than Q&A-style tutoring is a meaningful product distinction. *Region: United States.*

**Fermi.ai** -- AI STEM tutor built on Socratic methodology, primarily serving K-12 students. The founding team brings deep technical leadership experience, and the product reflects a disciplined focus on STEM subjects rather than trying to cover all domains. *Region: United States.*

**Bloom AI** -- AI tutor and teaching assistant using Socratic methods. Very early-stage, but worth watching as one of a growing number of entries from the Australian edtech ecosystem. *Region: Australia.*

## Instructional Design and Course Production

**BoodleBox** -- FERPA-compliant platform that gives institutions access to multiple large language models (GPT, Claude, Gemini) with custom bot creation and Canvas LMS integration. Interesting because it positions as infrastructure -- letting institutions adopt AI on their own terms rather than locking into a single model provider. *Region: United States.*

**Bevel** -- AI workspace purpose-built for instructional designers, with integrations across Canvas, Blackboard, and other learning management systems. Bootstrapped with strong product execution. The multi-LMS approach addresses a real pain point for institutions running heterogeneous tech stacks. *Region: United States.*

**Alma Learning** -- Full-stack instructional design platform incubated within a university environment. The university-incubated origin is notable -- it suggests the product was built close to actual faculty workflows rather than designed from the outside looking in. *Region: United States.*

---

## Assessment and Academic Integrity

**Coraltalk** -- AI-powered conversational assessment platform enabling oral exams, role-play scenarios, and structured dialogue-based evaluation. This is a direct response to the assessment-in-the-age-of-AI problem: if written work is unreliable, live conversation becomes a stronger signal of understanding. *Region: United States.*

---

## Educator Professional Development

**sideby** -- Peer-to-peer professional development platform for educators. Matches educators for AI-guided conversations about AI integration, then uses transcript analysis to surface evidence of learning for continuing education credits. Notable for a validated 2-year partnership with a state principals' association (68% badge completion rate vs. 5-15% typical for free online courses). Expanding through a 12-state leadership collaborative. *Region: United States.*

---

## Career Development and Workforce Readiness

**NexusEdge Education** -- AI-driven career development platform with employer-aligned micro-credentials, focused on community colleges. The community college focus is notable -- this is an underserved segment where career outcomes are the primary value proposition and institutional budgets are tight. *Region: United States.*

**Ediphi** -- Workforce-readiness platform entering through hospitality as its initial vertical. The vertical-specific wedge strategy makes sense for building credibility before expanding to broader workforce preparation. *Region: United States.*

---

## Student Engagement and Retention

**Reflectrr** -- Agentic retention engine where autonomous AI agents act on at-risk student signals rather than simply generating alerts for staff follow-up. This represents the next step in the retention technology arc: moving from dashboards to action. The shift from passive alerting to autonomous intervention is the most significant architectural change I observed in this category at the conference. *Region: United States.*

**COLAJ** -- Classroom engagement platform with a strong accessibility orientation, combining software with in-classroom hardware. The hardware component is unusual and potentially defensible -- most engagement tools are pure software. *Region: United States.*

---

## Other Notable

**Notey** -- Music education platform where the student's physical instrument serves as the game controller. A novel format that bridges digital engagement with real instrument practice. Worth noting as an example of creative hardware-software integration in an underserved subject area. *Region: United States.*
